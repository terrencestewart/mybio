Terrence has always aimed to combine technical know-how with great storytelling. He has worked as a producer at some of the most prestigious ad agencies in New York and Miami. He also has extensive experience working with global brands, including Verizon Wireless, Charmin, Carnival Cruise Lines and Bounty. Throughout the course of his career, he has served as the executive producer of a variety of material with celebrity talent, such as screen actor and legend James Earl Jones, Army Wives’ Catherine Bell, and Terry O’Quinn, from the hit TV series, Lost.

In 2005, Terrence joined the creative team at Publicis NY to begin working with Proctor & Gamble on two of their most profitable accounts. In 2007, he relocated to Miami, Florida to accept the position as “Director of Broadcast Production” at DDB. During this time, Terrence produced award-winning commercials for the Carnival Cruise Lines’ campaign, “The Fun Ships.” In 2009, he was the executive producer for How Sweet the Sound, a multi-million dollar annual event created and sponsored by Verizon Wireless. In addition, he also produced “The Ultimate Multi-Media Fan Experience” for the New York Jets/Giants stadium. Since 2010, he has won several honors, including a Gold Addy for Creativity and Outstanding Use of Technology. He also landed a Pixie Award for the prelaunch spot for the classic Hanes brand, L’eggs, in 2011. Terrence’s main objective is to create content with which people will want to engage in and share across multimedia platforms.
